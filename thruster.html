Thruster

Thruster is an Unity game developed entirely by me. It is an arcade racer with complex AI racers.


Basic Mechanics

  • The Player and AI are capable of moving forwards and backwards, as well as turning.
  • In order to take sharp corners racers can drift giving them a stronger turn.

Items

  • Both the player and AI can grab item boxes around the track in order to give them an advantage.
  • If the racers collect boxes of the same color, their currently held item upgrades.
  • AI racers will favor certain colored boxes based on whatever position they are in. For example, racers in the front will favor defensive and supportive items while racers in the back prefer attack items.

Race Logic

  • Maps are made up of checkpoints that must be passed in order to complete a lap. When a racer has hit all checkpoints their lap counter will increase by 1.
  • When determining what race position racers are in, internal logic will place racers based on how many laps and checkpoints the racer has completed. If racers have the same number of checkpoints and laps, it will put the racer closest to the current checkpoint in front.
  • When a race is completed by the player, racers will get points based on their position and their scores will carry onto the next race.
  • Along with racers saved points, AI personalities will also carry over to the next race.

AI Personalities

  • Before a race starts, the game will generate AI racers to compete against the player.
  • The name of a racer is made up of 2 parts, each part of the name is randomly selected from a JSON file and put together.
  • Racer colors are picked by selecting a primary, and a secondary color, then a tertiary color will be picked based off both the primary and secondary.
  • The skill of a racer determines how fast they are, before a race starts 1 racer will be given a very high speed and another is given a very low speed.

AI Pathing

  • Racers use Unity's built in navigation system and attempt to face an invisible box. Inputs are then fed using dot products using the same controller the player uses.
  • Racers also use invisible rays that detect if they are getting ready to collide with a wall or a racer and attempt to navigate away from them.
