Dungeon Bounties

Dungeon Bounties is an Unreal Engine 5 game developed entirely by me. It is an isometric procedural dungeon crawler with a complex inventory and upgrade system.


Dungeon Generator

  • Dungeons are generated one room at a time in a gridlike pattern. Once a room is picked and it doesn't collide with any other rooms, it is placed immediately.
  • Dungeon walls, floors, enemies, and props are picked from a bounty in order to make each dungeon feel unique.

Enemies

  • Enemies are generated with hats and outfits based on a bounty.
  • Enemies use a queue system so that they don't all attack at once and overwhelm the player.

Inventory System

  • The inventory system supports adding and removing items in bulk.
  • The player may also drag items around in order to shift their positioning.
  • If the player right clicks an item they can equip, destroy, or upgrade the item.
  • Inventory is saved between dungeon runs, and when the game is closed and reopened.

Loadouts

  • Players can equip multiple different armors, abilities, and weapons.
  • Armors provide stat bonuses that can increase various player stats.
  • Weapons change the attack animation of the player, and some can be used at a distance like guns, bows, or wands.
  • Abilities can be activated in order to provide a special effect for the player.

Bounties

  • Bounties are predined using Unreal Engine Data Tables allowing for quick and easy creation of new bounties.
  • Bounties define many attributes such as, what enemies spawn, the amount of rooms the dungeon creates, what kind of rooms can spawn, what the dungeon looks like, and what rewards the dungeon gives.
  • Bounty levels passively increase with the player based on how strong their equipment is, allowing for replayability.
